12 RESPONSIBLE CONSUMPTION AND PRODUCTION

So... What is SDGs Goal 12?

SDGs 12 is a Goal to ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns.

High-income countries have an environmental footprint 10 times larger per person than low-income countries, with fossil fuel subsidies doubling from $375 billion in 2020 to $732 billion in 2021. Additionally, each person wastes an average of 120 kg of food annually.

But fret not, from 2019 to 2022, 62 countries and the EU has already introduced 485 sustainable consumption policies, and company sustainability reporting has tripled since 2016.

Do you know that one of the most significant global waste is plastic?

Base on a report by the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), approximately 300 million tons of plastic waste are generated globally each year. Out of this, only about 9% is recycled, 12% is incinerated, and the remaining 79% ends up in landfills, dumps, or the environment.

What can we do to help reduce plastic wastage?

Everyone can help create a sustainable future. Choose reusable items over single-use plastics and recycle properly. Support policies reducing plastic production and join clean-up efforts like those by The Ocean Cleanup. Advocate for innovative solutions and support sustainable brands. These actions help achieve SDG Goal 12, leading to a cleaner, healthier environment for all.
